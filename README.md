# Verifiit Core Protocol

Verifiit is a blockchain-enabled product authentication and consumer accountability infrastructure designed to reduce counterfeit circulation and strengthen transparency in emerging markets.

## Overview

The Verifiit Protocol anchors product authentication events to a public proof-of-stake blockchain network, creating tamper-proof verification records accessible to multiple ecosystem stakeholders without centralized control.

The system combines:

- Secure product identifiers
- Consumer scan verification
- Cryptographic event hashing
- Smart contract-based authentication logging
- Incentivized participation mechanisms

## Problem

Counterfeit goods undermine consumer safety, brand trust, and retail accountability. Traditional centralized verification systems are vulnerable to tampering and lack cross-ecosystem transparency.

## Solution

Verifiit uses blockchain to:

- Record authentication events immutably
- Prevent post-event data manipulation
- Enable independent verification
- Support open Digital Public Goods infrastructure

Sensitive user data is stored off-chain. Only cryptographic hashes of verification events are anchored on-chain.

## Smart Contracts

Smart contracts are designed to:

- Register product batches
- Anchor scan verification events
- Log suspicious product flags
- Emit transparency events for auditability

## Digital Public Goods Alignment

Verifiit is being developed with the intention of contributing to the Digital Public Goods ecosystem. The project follows open-source principles and aims to meet the Digital Public Goods Standard, including open licensing, transparency, interoperability, and documentation.

The protocol is designed to support governments, NGOs, and consumer protection organizations in implementing trusted product authentication and transparency infrastructure in emerging markets.
